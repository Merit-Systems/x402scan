# Company Due Diligence

Conduct thorough due diligence on companies before making investment decisions.

---

## Company Overview Research

Build a comprehensive company profile.

```
Create a due diligence report on [Company X]:
- Company history and founding story
- Product and technology overview
- Business model and pricing
- Funding history and cap table info
- Team size and key hires
- Market positioning and competitors
```

**APIs Used:** Apollo Org Enrich → Firecrawl Scrape → Exa Search → Serper News → Apollo People Search

---

## Competitive Landscape Analysis

Understand where a company fits in the market.

```
Map the competitive landscape for [Company X]:
- Direct competitors and their funding
- How does the product differentiate?
- What do customers say in reviews?
- Market share indicators if available
```

**APIs Used:** Exa Search → Exa Find Similar → Apollo Org Enrich → Reddit Search → Grok X Search

---

## Team Quality Assessment

Evaluate the depth and quality of the team.

```
Assess the team at [Company X]:
- Leadership backgrounds and track records
- Engineering team size and composition
- Notable hires and where they came from
- Any key departures recently?
```

**APIs Used:** Apollo People Search → Apollo People Enrich → Clado LinkedIn Scrape → Serper News

---

## Market Validation Research

Gather evidence of product-market fit.

```
Find evidence of customer traction for [Company X]:
- Customer testimonials and case studies
- Reviews on G2, Capterra, or similar
- Social proof (tweets, Reddit mentions)
- Job postings that mention using them
```

**APIs Used:** Firecrawl Scrape (testimonials page) → Exa Search → Reddit Search → Grok X Search

---

## Risk Factor Identification

Uncover potential red flags or risks.

```
Research potential risks for investing in [Company X]:
- Any negative press or controversies?
- Glassdoor reviews and employee sentiment?
- Customer complaints on social media?
- Regulatory or legal concerns in their space?
```

**APIs Used:** Serper News → Exa Search → Reddit Search → Grok X Search → Firecrawl Search
